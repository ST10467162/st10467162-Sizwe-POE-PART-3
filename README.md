Name: QuickFix Connect

Organisation Description
QuickFix Connect is a small business website designed to connect people with trusted local service providers. Many locals have struggled in the past to find reliable service providers; it could be an AC repair technician, electrician, plumber, or home cleaning. They find the task stressful and very time consuming. They often succumb to issues of poor work quality, hidden charges, or unresponsive vendors. QuickFix Connect aims to solve this problem by creating one online platform where users can easily browse services, compare options, and book trusted professionals. The business focuses on convenience, reliability, and fast service delivery. (MC Vendor Hub, 2026)

QuickFix Connect have chosen to venture to an online marketplace place due to the undeniable benefits it comes with, offering organisations a streamlined approach to reach a vast target audience and enhance operations. The benefits are are:
1.	Increased Visibility 
•	An online marketplace would expose our services to millions of potential customers. For Example, Amazon have gained 300 million concurrent users, which is the highest exposure a seller can get. (Inriver, 2026)
2.	Cost-effective Setup
•	Unlike having to build our own infrastructure, joining an online marketplace requires a minimal upfront investment for the business to be up and running. (Inriver, 2026)
3.	Streamlined Operations 
•	Most marketplaces provide logistical support, such as shipping and returns. For example, the Fulfilment by Amazon (FBA), this allows sellers to outsource inventory management and distribution responsibilities. (Inriver, 2026)
4.	Access to Consumer Insights 
•	Providers gain data analytics-driven insights into customer behaviour, which aids in the improvement of pricing, inventory control, and marketing strategies. (Inriver, 2026)
The target audience includes homeowners, tenants, students, office workers, and anyone quick and affordable services. QuickFix Connect will make it easier for the target audience to find the help they need while also creating opportunities for local service providers to advertise their skills and grow their income.

Reason for Choosing the Organisation 
The main reason I choose to create QuickFix Connect is because of conveniency. Consumers are always eager to pay for a convenient experience since as the saying goes, "time is money.” Consumers are always willing to pay for ways to make their lives easier if they can save time and effort, which is why retailers and service providers always promote them. Consider Amazon for example, currently, two-thirds of the customers instead of getting in their cars and going to the store to buy what they want, they user the delivery service provided by Amazon because of one main reason “It is convenient.” Now imagine the lengths to what QuickFix Connect could achieve if we follow the same precedent, figuring out what consumer are lazy to do and capitalise on that fact by providing services that would satisfy their needs. (Bhattacharyya, 2022)

In conclusion, the consumers experience will always revolve around convenience, which is why it will be the center of QuickFix Connects strategy and objective. We would consider how we can make using our brand as simple and convenient as possible since in a world with little time and a plethora of alternatives, it would take more than the pricing of our services to beat our rivals. This however doesn't imply that we copy what our rivals are doing. We have a thorough understanding of both our target market and our consumers. (Bhattacharyya, 2022)








Website Goals and Objectives 
Marketing Goals
The marketing team handles promoting the website through advertising and campaigns. Their aim is to grow the business by presenting the website effectively and building a strong brand presence. They continuously work on increasing brand awareness and positioning the company as a top choice in the market. Common website marketing goals include: (Garg, 2024)

Lead Generation

Generating leads is a primary goal for marketers. A steady increase in leads shows that strategies are working well. This can be achieved by engaging visitors and presenting services in an appealing way. For example, a 20% increase in monthly leads compared to previous months shows satisfactory progress. (Garg, 2024)
•	Conversion Rate Improvement
-	Getting leads is not enough; those leads need to be converted into customers. If conversions are low, the website is not reaching its full potential. A realistic goal is to improve the conversion rate by around 5% each month. (Garg, 2024)
•	Brand Awareness
-	Making the target audience aware of your products or services is essential. Without awareness, it is difficult to attract traffic or generate leads. Businesses can aim for a 10% increase in awareness to support overall growth. (Garg, 2024)

Sales Goals
For QuickFix Connect, setting clear sales goals is crucial. The website acts as a communication channel between the sales team and customers, making effective interaction key. Typical sales goals include: (Garg, 2024)
1.	Increase Sales
•	Online businesses aim to grow their customer base quickly to boost sales. A practical goal is to increase sales by about 20% each month. (Garg, 2024)
2.	Sales Conversion
•	Not all orders placed are completed, some are cancelled. True sales conversion occurs when a customer finishes the purchase and completes payment. A 5% monthly improvement in completed sales is a reasonable target, achievable through attracting high-quality traffic. (Garg, 2024)
3.	Improved Sales Support
Providing dedicated support helps customers make confident purchasing decisions. This includes offering clear information, highlighting testimonials, and resolving doubts. Enhancing support can help increase the closing rate by about 5%. (Garg, 2024)

Customer Satisfaction Goals
Customer satisfaction is critical for long-term success. Satisfied customers are more likely to return and recommend the business. Key goals include:
1.	Customer Satisfaction Rate
Improving customer satisfaction helps reduce bounce rates and improves search rankings. Businesses should aim to increase satisfaction levels by around 10% each month, which can be measured through surveys and feedback. (Garg, 2024)
2.	Reduce Time to Complete Tasks
If users spend too much time on the website, it may show usability issues. The goal should be to streamline processes so customers can complete actions, like making a purchase, more quickly. (Garg, 2024)

IT Goals
The IT team focuses on supporting the technical performance of the website to ensure a smooth user experience. (Garg, 2024)
1.	Lower Website Management Costs
Managing a website involves ongoing maintenance and updates. IT teams should aim to reduce these costs by about 10%, possibly by using efficient content management systems and updating outdated technologies. (Garg, 2024)




KPIs to measure success
What are KPIs
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) in web analytics are measurable values that help track and evaluate how well a website or application is performing. These indicators are predefined and used to assess the success of marketing campaigns, strategies, or specific initiatives. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
KPIs also show how different actions contribute to overall business goals. By analyzing this data, businesses can adjust their strategies, improve performance, and make better decisions about where to invest time and resources. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
In addition, KPIs allow businesses and marketing teams to set benchmarks and create data-driven goals, making it easier to measure progress and improve website performance over time. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)

KPIs QuickFix Connect would track
1.	Unique Website Visitors
•	A unique website visitor refers to an individual who visits a website and interacts with one or more pages within a given time frame. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	Example: If a person visits the website and browses multiple pages, such as pricing, features, or blog pages, they are still counted as one unique visitor, not multiple. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)

2.	Traffic by source

•	Definition: Traffic by source is a KPI that shows where your website visitors are coming from. It helps find which channels or origins are driving traffic to the site. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
-	Traffic can be grouped into several categories, such as:
-	Devices (mobile, desktop, or app) 
-	Campaigns 
-	Platforms 
-	Channels (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	How it is measured:
-	Traffic by source is typically analysed using reporting or analytics tools, as calculating it manually is not practical. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	Why Traffic by Source Matters
-	Tracking traffic by source is important for marketing agencies because it helps them find which channels bring in the most visitors and which ones perform poorly. These sources can include social media, email campaigns, webinars, and others. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
-	It also offers deeper insights into the target audience, including where they come from and how they interact with the website, allowing for more informed marketing decisions. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)

3.	Sessions
•	 A session refers to a period during which a user interacts with a website. It starts when the user arrives on the site and ends when they leave. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	Example: If one user visits the website five separate times on different days using the same device or campaign, they are counted as one user but five sessions. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	If a user opens a new browser window and directly enters a page URL from the same visit, it is still considered part of the same session rather than a new one. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	How it is calculated:
-	Sessions can be analysed by dividing the total number of sessions over a specific period by the number of users during that same period. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	Why Sessions Matter
-	Tracking website sessions is valuable for marketing agencies because it helps evaluate the effectiveness of PPC, SEO, and other marketing strategies and experiments. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
-	It also provides insights into user behavior and patterns, making it easier to understand how visitors interact with the site. Additionally, sessions help map out the user journey and identify reasons why users leave the website, such as technical issues, bugs, or poor user experience. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)


4.	Conversion Rates
•	 The conversion rate measures the percentage of users who complete a desired action on a website, such as making a purchase, signing up, or filling out a form. A higher percentage reflects a more effective campaign. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	How it is calculated:
-	Divide the number of completed goals within a specific period by the total number of website users, then multiply the result by 100 to get the percentage. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	Why Conversion Rate Matters
-	Marketing agencies monitor conversion rates to understand how effectively they are encouraging users to take desired actions on a client’s website. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
-	It also helps in forecasting future sales and revenue more accurately. In addition, conversion rates provide valuable insights into how well campaign messaging resonates with the target audience. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)

5.	Average time on page
•	Definition:
 Average time on page is a KPI that measures how long, on average, visitors spend on a specific webpage. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	How it is calculated:
-	It is calculated by dividing the total time spent on a page by the number of pageviews, excluding exits. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	Why it matters:
-	This metric provides valuable insights for marketing agencies. A higher average time usually indicates that the content is engaging and relevant to visitors. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
-	However, if users spend too much time on pages like checkout, it may signal issues or confusion that need to be resolved. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
-	Since time spent on a page also impacts SEO performance, improving content quality can help increase engagement and overall website effectiveness. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)

6.	Bounce Rate
•	Definition:
 Bounce rate is the percentage of visitors who leave a website after viewing only one page without taking any further action. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	How it is calculated:
-	Divide the number of single-page visits (bounces) by the total number of visitors to the website or landing page, then multiply the result by 100 to get a percentage. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
•	Why it matters:
-	 Monitoring bounce rate helps marketers understand how users interact with individual pages on a website. This insight supports better optimization and more informed marketing decisions based on actual user behaviour. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)
-	It is also a key performance indicator because a high bounce rate may highlight issues in the marketing funnel or page experience that need to be fixed. (Vaičiūnaitė, 2022)

 CSS
CSS was used to style the website and improve its appearance. It was used to create the colour scheme, responsive layout, navigation menu, buttons, image galleries, service cards, forms, and overall visual design. CSS helps ensure the website is professional, user-friendly, and mobile responsive.
